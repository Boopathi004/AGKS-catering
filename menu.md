## 1. Marriage Ceremony (Kalyanam)
The grandest event in Tamil culture, requiring an elaborate, traditional multi-course feast served strictly on a **banana leaf** (*Vazhailai*) in a specific, ritualistic order.

### Functional Roles
* **Vadhyar (Iyer/Iyengar)**: Chief priest executing the holy rites, fire sacrifices, and *Saptapadi* (seven steps).
* **Mangala Isai Musicians**: Masters playing the *Nadaswaram* (traditional wind instrument) and *Thavil* (drums) to generate auspicious spiritual energy.
* **Mappillai Thozhan / Ponnu Thozhi**: The brother-in-law or sister-in-law assigned to guide the groom and bride through long rituals.
* **Vaasal Varavor**: Extended family members greeting guests at the entrance with *Paneer* (rose water), *Santhanam* (sandalwood paste), and *Kalkandu* (rock candy).
* **Parimaaruvor**: Experienced ceremonial servers who understand the strict geometric placement of food items on the leaf.

### The Grand Wedding Feast Menu (Kalyana Sappadu)
* **Welcome Drink**: Chilled rose milk or fresh watermelon juice.
* **Crisps**: Traditional *Appalam* (papad) and crunchy *Javvarisi Vadam* (sago crisps).
* **Sweet Starters**: *Asoka Halwa* or *Semolina Kesari* (always served first on the bottom-right of the leaf).
* **Poriyal (Dry Fry)**: Cabbage-carrot poriyal tossed with fresh grated coconut.
* **Varuval (Spicy Fry)**: Crispy, spicy *Seppankizhangu* (taro root) fry.
* **Kootu (Lentil Stew)**: Chow-chow (chayote) or yellow pumpkin kootu cooked with split moong dal.
* **Pachadi**: Sweet-sour pineapple pachadi or cucumber raita.
* **Main Rice Course 1**: Hot white rice topped with cooked *Paruppu* (moong dal) and a generous dollop of pure melted ghee.
* **Main Rice Course 2**: Traditional *Kalyana Sambar* packed with drumsticks, carrots, and small shallots.
* **Main Rice Course 3**: Tangy and intensely spicy *Vatha Kuzhambu* (sundried berry gravy).
* **Digestive Course**: Piping hot, peppery *Kalyana Rasam* poured over a fresh handful of rice.
* **Dessert**: Rich *Elaneer Payasam* (tender coconut milk pudding) or *Paruppu Pradhaman*.
* **Final Course**: Creamy curd rice paired with *Manga Oorugai* (spicy raw mango pickle) and *Mor Milagai* (sun-dried, salt-fried chiles).
* **Exit**: *Beeda* (sweet betel leaf paan) to aid digestion.

## 2. Baby Shower (Valaikaappu / Seemantham)
A warm, women-centric family function held during the odd months of pregnancy (5th, 7th, or 9th) to bless the expectant mother with protection and colorful glass bangles.

### Functional Roles
* **Sumangalis**: Married women who take turns adorning the mother-to-be's hands with bangles and applying *Nalangu* (turmeric paste).
* **Arathi Women**: Two senior women performing the protective *Arathi* ritual (crimson water wave) to ward off evil eyes from the pregnancy.
* **Kadhai Solluni**: Maternal elders passing down child-rearing wisdom, blessing verses, and traditional folk songs.

### The Traditional Variety Rice Menu (Kalandha Sadham)
By tradition, a *Valaikaappu* feast must feature an odd number of vibrant, uniquely flavored variety rices (usually 5, 7, or 9 types) to satisfy the mother's pregnancy cravings.

* **Elumichai Sadham**: Zesty lemon rice tempered with peanuts, mustard seeds, and green chiles.
* **Puliyodharai**: Tangy, deeply spiced temple-style tamarind rice cooked with ground spice powders.
* **Thengai Sadham**: Fragrant coconut rice garnished with roasted cashews and crispy curry leaves.
* **Karuveppilai Sadham**: Healthy, iron-rich curry leaf pesto rice.
* **Ellu Sadham**: Earthy, roasted black sesame seed rice with a hint of sesame oil.
* **Sakkarai Pongal**: Rich jaggery, milk, and ghee sweet rice pudding.
* **Thakali Sadham**: Spicy, savory tomato masala rice.
* **Sides**: Crunchy *Urulaikizhangu Varuval* (spicy roasted potato cubes) and *Kootu Vadam* (rice crackers).
* **Finishing**: Simple, unseasoned *Thayir Sadham* (curd rice) with mustard tempering to completely soothe the stomach.

## 3. Child's First Birthday & Ear Piercing (Aayushya Homam & Kaadhukuthu)
A vital milestone celebrated either at ancestral family temples or community halls to pray for the longevity, health, and spiritual well-being of the toddler.

### Functional Roles
* **Aachaari**: The traditional goldsmith who expertly pierces the child's ears using sterile gold pins or wire.
* **Thaai Maaman**: The maternal uncle who holds the child securely on his lap, comforting them during the piercing ceremony.
* **Moi Ezhuthuvor**: A dedicated relative tasked with recording monetary blessings (*Moi*) and gift details in a formal family ledger book.

### The Comforting Feast Menu
* **Sweet**: *Paal Payasam* (traditional slow-reduced milk and rice pudding).
* **Breakfast / Tiffin Items (If morning)**: Pillowy-soft *Kushboo Idlis*, crispy fried *Medhu Vada*, and flaky *Mini Ghee Roast Dosas*.
* **Chutneys**: Spicy red tomato chutney and fresh ground white coconut chutney.
* **Main Lunch**: Steamed white rice served with aromatic *Arachuvitta Sambar* (made with freshly roasted and ground whole spices).
* **Side Dishes**: *Urulaikizhangu Podimas* (mildly mashed tempered potatoes) and *Avial* (mixed vegetables cooked in a rich coconut-yogurt gravy).
* **Digestive**: Traditional *Tamarind Rasam* and smooth curd.

## 4. Housewarming Ceremony (Grihapravesam)
An early morning spiritual event performed well before sunrise to purify a newly constructed or freshly leased home from negative energies.

### Functional Roles
* **Gho Poojai Operators**: Handlers who safely guide a sacred cow and her young calf into the house first to channel cosmic blessings.
* **Milk Boilers**: The matriarch of the house who supervises the symbolic boiling over of fresh milk on the kitchen stove to welcome abundance.
* **Ganapathi Homam Priests**: Spiritual experts setting up the sacred fire altar in the main living room to chant cleansing mantras.

### The Early Morning Tiffin Menu
Because this function concludes early in the morning right after sunrise, a hearty, warm South Indian breakfast is served to the guests.

* **Welcome Drink**: Strong, frothy South Indian filter coffee served in traditional brass *Dabarah* cups.
* **Auspicious Starter**: *Ven Pongal* (savory ghee, black pepper, cumin, and ginger rice) served steaming hot.
* **Main Bread**: Fluffy, golden-fried *Poori* paired with savory *Kizhangu Masala* (spiced potato and onion mash).
* **Steamed Goods**: Small, soft *Ghee Mini Idlis* floating in a deep bowl of hot sambar.
* **Crunch**: Crispy, golden-brown *Uzhunthu Vadai* (black gram lentil fritters).
* **Chutney Duo**: Tangy mint-coriander green chutney and classic white coconut chutney.
* **Dessert**: Bright orange *Rava Kesari* melting with ghee and loaded with raisins.
